# ICT Competence – Digital Application
### UNESCO ICT-CFT · Teacher Training with mBot

---

> **Course context:** This module is part of a teacher training programme exploring how the UNESCO ICT Competency Framework for Teachers (ICT-CFT) can be applied in robotics-based learning with the **mBot**.

---

**Learning Goals**

After completing this module, participants will be able to:

- Distinguish the three levels of digital integration: *Application → Infusion → Transformation*
- Describe what **Transformation** means in a pedagogical sense
- Design at least one mBot activity that goes **beyond mere application** and reaches the transformation level

---

## 1 · The UNESCO ICT-CFT in Brief

The **UNESCO ICT Competency Framework for Teachers** defines six competency areas across three progressive levels of depth:

| Level | Core Idea |
|---|---|
| **Knowledge Acquisition** | Teachers use ICT to support traditional instruction |
| **Knowledge Deepening** | Teachers use ICT to apply knowledge in complex, real-world situations |
| **Knowledge Creation** | Teachers model and support students as knowledge creators |

<div class="highlight-box">
🎯 <strong>Focus of this module:</strong> The competency area <em>Digital Skills Application</em> — specifically how it moves through the three levels, culminating in <strong>Transformation</strong>.
</div>

---

## 2 · Three Levels of Digital Skills Application

> Moving from "using technology" to "transforming learning"

<!-- style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr 1fr; gap:1em;" -->
| 🖥️ Application | 🔗 Infusion | 🚀 Transformation |
|---|---|---|
| Teachers and students **use digital tools** (e.g. presentations, learning platforms, search engines) | Digital media are **firmly embedded** in lessons and learning processes | Digital competences **enable entirely new forms of learning** that would be impossible without technology |
| *Same lesson, digital format* | *Digital tools are routine, not special* | *Learning itself is redesigned* |

<div class="transformation-box">
💡 <strong>Key insight:</strong> Transformation is not about more technology — it is about <em>pedagogical change enabled by technology</em>. The mBot is not a gadget; it is a catalyst for a new type of lesson.
</div>

---

## 3 · What Does Transformation Look Like?

**Transformation = pedagogical change, not just technical progress**

When teachers reach the Transformation level, they redesign learning by enabling:

**1. New forms of learning organisation**

- Self-directed learning
- Personalised learning paths
- Project-oriented work
- Collaborative problem-solving

**2. New learning experiences only possible with technology**

- Virtual cooperation with schools worldwide
- Simulations and virtual labs
- AI-supported learning assistance
- Creating own media products (podcasts, videos, apps)

**3. A changed role for the teacher**

| ❌ Traditional role | ✅ Transformed role |
|---|---|
| Knowledge transmitter | Learning facilitator |
| Instructor | Coach & Moderator |
| Expert | Innovator |

---

## 4 · mBot in Teacher Training – From Application to Transformation

> How can the mBot help teachers experience all three levels — and reach Transformation?

<div class="mbot-box">
🤖 The <strong>mBot</strong> (Makeblock) is a programmable robot for educational use. It supports block-based (mBlock/Scratch) and text-based (Python/Arduino) coding and has sensors for light, distance, and line-following.
</div>

**Activity Progression across ICT-CFT Levels**

| Level | mBot Activity Example |
|---|---|
| **Application** | Trainer demonstrates mBot driving in a straight line; participants watch a tutorial and replicate it — *technology used to illustrate a concept* |
| **Infusion** | Participants systematically programme sensor reactions (line-following, obstacle avoidance) as part of a structured coding sequence — *digital tool firmly integrated in the learning flow* |
| **Transformation** | Participants design a **collaborative challenge project**: teams programme the mBot to solve a real-world problem scenario (e.g. navigate a mock hospital floor plan to "deliver medicine"), then present, compare and improve each other's solutions — *learning is restructured; teacher becomes coach* |

---

## 5 · Your mBot Transformation Activity – Design Template

> Use this framework to plan a lesson that reaches the Transformation level.

**Step-by-step planning guide**

**① Define the challenge scenario**

> Describe a real or realistic problem your mBot should solve (not just "make it drive").

Example: *"The mBot must navigate a city grid, stop at red lines (traffic lights), and find the shortest route between two points."*

---

**② Assign collaborative roles**

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Navigator | Plans the route logic |
| Programmer | Writes the mBlock code |
| Tester | Runs the robot and logs errors |
| Presenter | Documents and presents results |

---

**③ Embed reflection & iteration**

- After each test run: *What worked? What did not? Why?*
- Build in at least **two iteration cycles**
- Final step: peer evaluation between groups

---

**④ Ask: Are we at Transformation level?**

<div class="highlight-box">
✅ <strong>Check:</strong> Could this learning experience happen the same way without the mBot and without digital tools? <br/>
If the answer is <em>No</em> — you have reached <strong>Transformation</strong>.
</div>

---

**Key Takeaway**

> The mBot is most powerful not when it is a demonstration object, but when it becomes the **medium through which students collaborate, create, and solve problems** — changing the role of the teacher from instructor to learning guide.
