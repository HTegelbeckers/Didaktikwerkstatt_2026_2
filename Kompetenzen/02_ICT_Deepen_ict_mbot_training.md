## 📋 Slide 1 – What is the Infusion Level?

> **Aspect 4 – Application of Digital Skills: Infusion**
>
> At this level, teachers go beyond simply *using* technology — they **blend** it purposefully into learning to promote **critical thinking, creativity, and problem-solving**.

---

### Key Competencies (KD.4.a–g)

| Code | Competency |
|------|-----------|
| **KD.4.a** | Operate subject-appropriate software (incl. VR/AR & simulations) |
| **KD.4.b** | Evaluate accuracy & usefulness of web-based tools |
| **KD.4.c** | Use authoring tools to design curriculum materials |
| **KD.4.d** | Use school management software |
| **KD.4.e** | Use digital communication tools for collaboration |
| **KD.4.f** | Use interlinked digital devices for sharing & collaboration |
| **KD.4.g** | Source tools for students with disabilities & diverse backgrounds |

> 🔑 **Core Idea:** Technology is not just a tool — it becomes an *integrated part* of how students **think, explore, and create**.

## 🤖 Slide 2 – Why mBot?

> **mBot** (by Makeblock) is a programmable educational robot designed for STEM learning. It combines **hardware** (sensors, motors, LEDs) with **software** (mBlock/Scratch-based coding) — making it an ideal *Infusion-level* tool.

## 💡 Slide 4 – Example Activities

> These activities show how mBot transforms abstract competencies into **hands-on classroom experiences** across subjects.

---

### Activity 1 – "The Robot Cartographer" 🗺️
*Subjects: Math / Geography | KD.4.a, KD.4.c*

    {{0-1}}
> **Task:** Students program mBot to navigate a grid map scaled to real distances.  
> They write the movement algorithm, test it, and debug.  
> **Higher-order thinking:** Spatial reasoning, proportional thinking, iterative design

```
Example mBlock pseudocode:
  move forward 2 steps  (= 20 cm)
  turn right 90°
  move forward 1 step   (= 10 cm)
  play buzzer tone C
```

---

### Activity 2 – "Sense & Respond" 🔦
*Subjects: Science / Physics | KD.4.b, KD.4.f*

    {{1-2}}
> **Task:** Use mBot's ultrasonic sensor to measure distances and log data.  
> Students compare sensor readings vs. manual measurements.  
> **Higher-order thinking:** Accuracy, evaluation, data literacy

---

### Activity 3 – "Inclusive Robot Race" ♿
*All subjects | KD.4.g*

    {{2-3}}
> **Task:** Teams design a robot race course where *all* students participate.  
> Students with motor difficulties use switch-adapted input or voice commands.  
> **Higher-order thinking:** Empathy, design thinking, accessibility awareness

---

### Activity 4 – "mBot News Broadcast" 📡
*Subjects: Language / Media | KD.4.e, KD.4.c*

    {{3}}
> **Task:** Students program mBot to "present" results of an experiment (LED signals, sounds).  
> They author a digital report or video to share with other classes via school intranet.  
> **Higher-order thinking:** Communication, authoring, digital citizenship
